Technical Positions

August 2016 - current: Software Development Engineer, Amazon

I've recently commenced work for Amazon as an SDE in the RDS Aurora team.

September 2010 - July 2016: Software Developer, IBM

I had the following major responsibility areas within IBM:
  • Development of the distributed transactions facility in WebSphere Application Server and WebSphere Application Server Liberty Profile.
  • Architecture and development of an RDBMS-backed triple store and ORM, along with performance of the Java application built on the store. The ORM worked across Oracle, DB2, and SQL Server, giving me development experience across all three systems.
  • Development of an OSLC data server. This was heavily based on linked data technologies, and my skills in this area proved valuable to the team.

Prior to my move into the WebSphere Application Server team, I was responsible within my department for any work involving database systems. This involved regular client interaction as well as highly technical development tasks. In addition, my work on application performance yielded substantial improvements, and pre-empted several customer issues.

I have a strong reputation for high quality, reliable code, and build on this by taking an active role in enhancing functional and performance testing capabilities.

July 2008 – October 2008: Intern, HP Labs

I spent four months at HP Labs Bristol during the course of my Ph.D., where I was responsible for designing and implementing a clustered prototype of Jena TDB, their existing RDF database.

July 2005 – June 2006: Software Developer, Fidessa group plc

At Fidessa I was responsible for London Stock Exchange market data, including testing and deployment of a major rewrite of the software. This reinforced my understanding of operating in an environment where high code reliability is essential.

Summer 2004 & Summer 2003: Research Assistant, University of Southampton

During the summers of 2004 and 2003 I worked on the EQUATOR multidisciplinary research project at the University of Southampton. In 2004, my work involved reading vector format plans of buildings and, with the minimum possible human guidance, assign meaning to the data. In 2003 I worked on collation of data about the many sub-projects under the umbrella of EQUATOR, and the publication of that data in RDF format as preparation for the project's half-completion review. I was successful in this task and credited with greatly easing the review process.
